About

Who I was before?

Natural scientist, animal researcher, vole researcher, large mammal researcher, project researcher, and animal ecologist. Many options - one man. Personally I prefer to be called 'bootleg biologist' (saapasjalkabiologi in Finnish).

I am from Oulainen, Northern Ostrobothnia. I completed statutory school and high school in my city of birth. I went to study animal ecology into the University of Oulu. During bachelor's degree I specialized in environmental protection and habitat selection and social information use of birds. In 2016 I finished a master’s degree, specializing in animal ecology and morphological variation of moose's jawbone in the geographical landscape. After the studies, I have been working as a project researcher at the University of Jyväskylä researching predator-prey interactions of bank vole and least weasel. I have also studied the effect of noise and light pollution on the great tit (Parus major) in the Netherlands Institute of Ecology (NIOO-KNAW).

What Now?

In July 2020 while writing this, I am wrapping up master-level studies in Software Engineering at the University of Jyväskylä. I started in January 2019. According to my current study plan, I aim to graduate in the late spring of 2021. This summer I will finish the last normal courses and apart from those I will have a one special programming task and a master thesis that I should still attend to. Meanwhile studying, I am keeping eyes and ears wide open for job opportunities at the moment.

My Dream Job

This one is not so clear to me yet. There are so many interesting career paths in IT. Being part of a software developing team is one of my aims. I want to contribute by working hard and seeing the results of my own work. Doesn't matter if results are to be seen in the form of well-written program code, fresh ideas, or bugs found through software testing or all of these combined. The results are results. As long as I can contribute to increasing the overall quality of the software under development. Furthermore, I definitely would not mind having some kind of cybersecurity/system vulnerability aspect on my job either. After all, maintaining high standards in data and system security is essential for one to meet the requirements of any software developing project.
